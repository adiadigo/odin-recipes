Vada Pao


Description

Vada pao is a vegetarian dish originating from the Indian state of Maharashtra. It is widely popular in the Indian subcontinent as well as in the Western world. It is made with a deep-fried potato patty (vada) sandwiched between a pav (bread roll) along with a spicy garlic chutney. It is a popular street food in India.

Ingredients

  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 6 pav (bread rolls)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. When they start to splutter, add chopped onions, green chilies, and ginger. Saute until the onions turn golden brown.
  2. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add mashed potatoes and mix well. Cook for 5-7 minutes on low heat.
  4. Shape the potato mixture into small round patties and deep fry until golden brown.
  5. Slit the pav (bread rolls) and spread garlic chutney on one side.
  6. Place the vada (potato patty) inside the pav and serve hot.
